About Period Closing

What is Period Closing?

Period closing locks an accounting period (usually a month) to prevent any further changes to transactions dated in that period. This ensures data integrity and supports audit requirements.

Before Closing a Period:

  • ✅ All journal entries must be posted (no drafts allowed)
  • ✅ Trial Balance must be balanced (debits = credits)
  • ✅ All required reports should be generated and reviewed
  • ✅ BIR reports filed (if applicable)

After Closing:

  • 🔒 No transactions can be created, edited, or deleted in the closed period
  • 📊 Financial reports remain accurate and unchangeable
  • ✅ Data integrity is maintained for audit purposes
  • 🔓 Period can be reopened by administrators if needed (with audit trail)
